Jun Zeng

zengjunsjtu AT berkeley DOT edu / Google Scholar / GitHub

I'm currently a Ph.D. student in the Department of Mechanical Engineering at UC Berkeley, with my research interest on robotics science. At UC Berkeley, I focus on the control, planning, optimization, learning about aerial-manipulation in the Hybrid Robotics Lab, with professor Koushil Sreenath.

On the academic side, I'm interested in the challenging problems in robotics associated with complex vehicle dynamics. When the system interact with the environments, surrounding obstacles, planning through contact, safety criteria should be considered, under the constraints of hardware performance and software compatibility. To tackle these problems, model-based methods such as geometric control, model predictive control, hybrid systems theory, barrier functions, computational motion planning, and optimization and deep learning techniques could be applied. We hope to create the safety-critical with optimal guarantees while not just simply restricting robots with conservative policies.

On the engineering side, I would like to think about other general topics about robotics. Specially, I enjoy programming with collaboration with other colleagues.

Previously, I have worked on autonomous truck at Plus AI, diffrential equations and numerical simulation at IFSTTAR/Dassault Systèmes, statistical learning and mapping at École Nationale Supérieure de Techniques Avancées, simulink development at Valeo Automotive Electrical Systems.

In the spare time, I like to read modern literature and enjoy cooking.

Publications

Differential Flatness based Path Planning with Direct Collocation on Hybrid Modes for a Quadrotor with a Cable-Suspended Payload
Jun Zeng, Prasanth Kotaru, Mark Mueller, Koushil Sreenath, ICRA/RA-L 2020 [paper] [video]

We use a coordinate-free geometric formulation and exploit a differential flatness based hybrid model of a quadrotor with a cable-suspended payload. We perform direct collocation on the differentially-flat hybrid system, and use complementarity constraints to avoid specifying hybrid mode sequences. The non-differentiable obstacle avoidance constraints are reformulated using dual variables, resulting in smooth constraints. We show that our approach has lower computational time than the state-of-the-art and guarantees feasibility of the trajectory with respect to both the system dynamics and input constraints without the need to tune lots of parameters.

Geometric Control and Differential Flatness of a Quadrotor UAV with Load Suspended from a Pulley
Jun Zeng, Prasanth Kotaru, Koushil Sreenath, ACC 2019 [paper]

We study a quadrotor with a cable-suspended load, where the cable length can be controlled by applying a torque on a pulley attached to the quadrotor. A nonlinear geometric controller is developed, that enables tracking of outputs defined by either (a) quadrotor attitude, (b) load attitude, (c) load position and cable length.

Geometric Control of a Quadrotor with a Load Suspended from an Offset
Jun Zeng, Koushil Sreenath, ACC 2019 [paper]

A quadrotor with a point-mass payload suspended with an offset from the center-of-mass of the quadrotor to the suspension point is studied in this paper. The coupling dynamics is analyzed and a new geometric controller is proposed to improve the tracking performance.
