Jun Zeng

zengjunsjtu AT berkeley DOT edu / Google Scholar / GitHub

I'm currently a Ph.D. student in the Department of Mechanical Engineering at UC Berkeley, with my interest focus on Robotics and Control. At UC Berkeley, I focus on the research in control, planning, optimization about aerial-manipulation, with professor Koushil Sreenath.

On the academic side, I'm interested in challenging problems in robotics associated with complex vehicle dynamics. When the system interact with the environments, the surrounding obstacles, planning through contact, safety criteria should be considered, under the constraints of hardware performance and software compatibility. To tackle these problems, model-based methods such as geometric control, hybrid systems theory, barrier functions, computational motion planning, and optimization and deep learning techniques could be applied. We hope to create the safety-critical with optimal guarantees while not just simply restricting robots with conservative policies.

On the engineering side, I would like to think about other general topics about robotics. Specially, I enjoy programming with collaboration with other colleagues.

In the spare time, I like to read modern literature and enjoy cooking a lot.

Publications

Differential Flatness based Path Planning with Direct Collocation on Hybrid Modes for a Quadrotor with a Cable-Suspended Payload
Jun Zeng, Prasanth Kotaru, Mark Mueller, Koushil Sreenath, RA-L 2020

We use a coordinate-free geometric formulation and exploit a differential flatness based hybrid model of a quadrotor with a cable-suspended payload. We perform direct collocation on the differentially-flat hybrid system, and use complementarity constraints to avoid specifying hybrid mode sequences. The non-differentiable obstacle avoidance constraints are reformulated using dual variables, resulting in smooth constraints. We show that our approach has lower computational time than the state-of-the-art and guarantees feasibility of the trajectory with respect to both the system dynamics and input constraints without the need to tune lots of parameters.
